## 1. TAXPAYER IDENTIFICATION NUMBER (TIN) REGISTRATION

### Overview
The TIN is a unique computer-generated number issued to each taxpayer (Individual, Business, Government Department, or Special Body). The GRA is the only authorized agency that issues TINs.

### Application Methods
1. **Online Application:** Submit through the eServices portal (https://eservices.gra.gov.gy/)
2. **In-Person Application:** Visit a GRA office

### Required Documents for Individuals

**Primary Requirements:**
- Original and copy of valid identification:
  - Passport, OR
  - National Identification Card, OR
  - National Insurance Scheme Card
- Original and copy of proof of address:
  - Utility bills
  - Bank statement
  - Mail received via post
- Completed TIN application form

**Special Cases:**
- If proof of address is not in the applicant's name, the person to whom the statements apply must provide:
  - Valid copy of identification
  - Testimony form

**Fee Exemptions:**
- Pensioners or persons aged 65 and older: No fee required

### Required Documents for Sole Traders
- Sole traders can use their Individual TIN for business registration
- If no Individual TIN exists:
  - Copy of Business Registration from Commercial Registry (if registered)

### Required Documents for Partnerships
- Valid identification from all Partners (if not already possessing Individual TIN)
- Completed Taxpayer Registration Form
- Note: Generally not required unless applying for VAT Registration

### Required Documents for Companies/Organizations
- Copy of Identification Card/Passport/TIN for Director(s) and Secretary
- Business/Company/Friendly Society certificate
- Proof of Address (utility bill, bank statement, or mail received via post)

### Important Notes
- Application fee must be paid within one month
- Failure to collect certificate within one month may require re-submission
- Power of Attorney required if applicant cannot visit GRA office personally

## 2. INDIVIDUAL INCOME TAX REQUIREMENTS

### Filing Threshold
Individuals whose income exceeds the applicable yearly threshold must file a return, unless the income is subject to withholding tax.

### Filing Deadline
- Standard deadline: **April 30** of each year
- **2025 Extension:** Deadline extended to **May 9, 2025** for Year of Assessment 2025

### Who Must File
Any individual who:
- Earned income in Guyana
- Derived income from Guyana
- Exercised employment in Guyana (taxed on total income whether received in Guyana or not)
- Residents must also report income from outside Guyana

### Filing Methods

#### Method 1: Electronic Submission (eServices)
1. Create an eServices account at https://eservices.gra.gov.gy/
2. Login to account
3. Select "Submit Return" option
4. Fill in return details
5. Upload relevant documents
6. Submit

#### Method 2: Manual (Paper) Submission
1. Download and fill in Income Tax Return form (Form G0004)
2. Print and sign the form
3. Attach all required supporting documents
4. Drop off at GRA location or designated tax site

### Required Documents for Individual Income Tax

**For Employed Persons (PAYE):**
- Form 7B slip (signed and stamped by employer)
- Medical/life insurance claims: Premium letter from Insurance Provider

**For MIR Taxpayers:**
- Form 4 from financial institution

**For Self-Employed Persons:**
- Financial Statement:
  - Balance Sheet
  - Income & Expenditure Statement
  - Cash Flow Statement
- Wear & Tear Schedule (if claiming Wear & Tear)
- Loss Schedule or Loss Relief Schedule (if applicable)
- Financial Statement Summary (Form G0017 and G0018)

**Additional Requirements:**
- All spaces in the form must be completed (write "None", "-", or "0" if not applicable)
- Declaration section must be filled out and signed
- Self-employed accounts must be prepared on an accrual basis

### Quarterly Payment Requirements (Self-Employed)
Payments due on or before:
- April 1
- July 1
- October 1
- December 31

## 3. CORPORATE INCOME TAX (CIT) REQUIREMENTS

### Who Must File
Any entity (not being an individual) that is registered in or carried on business in Guyana must file a corporation tax return.

### Tax Residency
- **Resident Companies:** Taxed on worldwide income
- **Non-Resident Companies:** Taxed only on income derived from Guyana

### Filing Deadline
- **April 30** of each year (or next business day if April 30 is a holiday/weekend)
- **2025 Extension:** May 9, 2025

### Corporate Tax Rates
- **Commercial Companies:** 40% of chargeable profits OR 2% MCT of turnover (whichever is higher)
- Commercial company definition: Derives at least 75% of gross income from goods not manufactured by it, or engaged in telecommunication, banking, or insurance

### Advance Tax Payments
Quarterly instalments based on preceding year's tax liability, due:
- March 15
- June 15
- September 15
- December 15

### Required Documents
- Corporation Tax Return (completed)
- Profit and Loss Statement
- Balance Sheet
- Income and Expenditure Statement
- All required supporting documents

### Record Keeping
- Must maintain proper accounts and records
- Retention period: Minimum **8 years** after completion of transactions

### Penalties for Non-Compliance
- Failure to file and pay by April 30: **10%** additional charge on outstanding tax
- Failure to pay balance by April 30: **2% per month** penalty plus **18% per annum** interest
- Loss/deficit returns submitted after deadline: **GYD 50,000** flat fee

### Tax Audits
- Companies selected at random
- Frequency: Generally every **3 years**
- Must provide financial information and supporting documentation

## 4. VALUE ADDED TAX (VAT) REQUIREMENTS

### VAT Threshold
**GYD 15,000,000** in taxable activity

### Mandatory Registration
Required when:
- Taxable activity equals or exceeds GYD 15,000,000 at end of 12 months
- Taxable activity exceeds threshold in less than 12 months
- Taxable activity expected to exceed threshold in next 12 months
- Mandated by Commissioner-General

### Voluntary Registration
Available if turnover is below GYD 15,000,000 threshold

**Eligibility Criteria:**
- Business and business location must be easily identifiable
- Ability to maintain records per Section 60 of VAT Act
- Demonstrate intention to make taxable supplies

### Required Documents for VAT Registration
- Copy of Identification Card/Passport/TIN for Director(s) and Secretary
- Business/Company/Friendly Society certificate
- Proof of Address (utility bill, bank statement, or mail received via post)

**For New Businesses or Those Operating Less Than One Year:**
- Evidence of bank loans and revenue projections
- Contracts or details of arrangements to make taxable supplies
- Feasibility studies
- Purchase of capital equipment documentation

### VAT Certificate
- Issued upon successful registration
- Must be displayed conspicuously at business location

### Penalties for Non-Registration
**Double the amount** of output tax payable from the time registration was required until application is filed

## 5. PAYE (PAY AS YOU EARN) REQUIREMENTS

### Who Must File
Every employer or person paying remuneration (including Government) must prepare and submit returns for each employee.

### Filing Requirements

**Monthly Returns:**
- **Form 5:** Return of Deductions of Tax by an Employer
- Due: **14th day** of each month following the month of payment
- Must be submitted together with remission of applicable taxes

**Annual Summary:**
- **Form 2:** Return to be made by Employers of Persons employed by them
  - Form 2 Summary Sheet
  - Form 2 Spreadsheet
  - Form 2 Declaration
- Due: **February 28** in the year of assessment

### Remittance Requirements
- Employers must deduct and withhold income tax per Section 93(1) of Income Tax Act
- Remittance to Commissioner-General: Within **14 days** of end of every calendar month

## 6. WITHHOLDING TAX REQUIREMENTS

### Who Must Withhold Tax

**Rental Income:**
- Tenants or agents collecting rents for non-resident landlords
- Persons renting tangible/intangible assets from non-residents

**Business Payments:**
- Franchise holders
- Management charges to non-residents
- Technical and professional services to non-residents
- Financial institutions paying interest on interest-bearing accounts
- Non-resident company offices/agencies deemed to remit profits to parent company

### Filing Requirements
- **Electronic submission required**
- Due: **14th day** of the month following the month WHT was deducted
- Format: Comma-separated values (CSV) with comma separator
- Submit via GRA eServices or through tax agent
- One file per month

### 2% Resident Contractor Withholding Tax
- Deducted by contractee from payment to contractor
- Applies to **RESIDENT CONTRACTORS only**
- Remittance deadline: **30 days** from date of deduction

### Penalties
**10% penalty** plus interest for failure to deduct, withhold, remit, or pay amounts owed to GRA

## 7. PROPERTY TAX REQUIREMENTS

### Filing Threshold
Net property value exceeding **GYD 40,000,000** on December 31 of any year

### Filing Deadline
**April 30** of the following year

### Who Must File
Every individual (resident or non-resident) possessing net property exceeding the threshold

### Tax Rate
- 0.5% to 0.75% on every dollar above GYD 40,000,000
- No tax if property value is below GYD 40,000,000

## 8. CERTIFICATE OF COMPLIANCE (LAND/PROPERTY TRANSFER)

### When Required
For any change of property ownership by:
- Sale
- Transfer
- Gift
- Lease

### Legal Requirement
Required per:
- Section 16(A)(a) of Deeds Registry Act, Chapter 5:01
- Section 76(2)(a) of Land Registry Act, Chapter 5:02

### Required Documents

**Tax Compliance:**
- All Income Tax Returns for last **8 years**
- All Corporate Tax Returns for last **8 years**
- All Property Tax Returns for last **8 years**
- Payment of all taxes for last **8 years**
- Tax returns for both Individual and Company Property Tax

**Transaction Details:**
- Details of expenses incurred on transaction
- Copies of all Legal Fee receipts (in Donor's name, if applicable)
- Documentary evidence of capital improvements after acquisition
- Evidence of other expenses incurred in transaction

**Power of Attorney (if applicable):**
- Updated Power of Attorney
- All supporting documents

### Applicable Taxes
Applicant must submit returns and pay taxes (or arrange payment) for:
- Income Tax
- Property Tax
- PAYE
- VAT
- Corporation Tax
- Customs Duties
- Excise Tax
(Whichever applicable)

### Certificate Validity
Expires on **April 30** following the date of issue

### Application Process
1. Visit Taxpayer Services Unit at GRA (200-201 Camp Street) or Regional Branch Office
2. Submit application form (signed by Donor or Power of Attorney) and supporting documents
3. Alternatively, email to: compliance@gra.gov.gy
4. Attend interview at GRA
5. Wait for notification when to return

## 13. PENALTIES AND COMPLIANCE

### Late Filing Penalties
- **Individual/Corporate Returns:** 10% charge on outstanding tax if not filed by April 30
- **Loss/Deficit Returns:** GYD 50,000 flat fee

### Late Payment Penalties
- **2% per month** penalty on outstanding balance
- **18% per annum** interest on outstanding balance

### Non-Registration Penalties
- **VAT:** Double the amount of output tax payable from required registration date

### Withholding Tax Non-Compliance
- **10%** penalty plus interest for failure to remit

### General Non-Compliance
- Refusal to accept returns if supporting documents not submitted
- Commissioner-General may determine chargeable income and assess accordingly

## 15. BEST PRACTICES FOR COMPLIANCE

### Record Keeping
- Maintain records for minimum **8 years**
- Keep digital and physical copies of all submissions
- Organize by tax year and type
- Store supporting documentation with returns

### Timely Filing
- Set reminders for monthly, quarterly, and annual deadlines
- File early to avoid last-minute issues
- Use electronic filing when possible for faster processing

### Documentation
- Keep all receipts and invoices
- Maintain detailed financial records
- Document all business transactions
- Organize employee records systematically

### Professional Assistance
- Consider using tax agents for complex returns
- Consult GRA Tax Advisory Services when uncertain
- Attend GRA taxpayer education sessions

### Digital Adoption
- Create eServices account early
- Download and use Padna mobile app
- Keep account credentials secure
- Regularly check for GRA updates and announcements
