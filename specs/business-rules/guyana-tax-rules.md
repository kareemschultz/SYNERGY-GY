## 1. Income Tax

### 1.1 Personal Income Tax

#### Tax Rates and Thresholds (2025 Tax Year)

| Annual Income (GYD) | Tax Rate |
|---------------------|----------|
| First 780,000 | 0% |
| 780,001 - 1,560,000 | 28% |
| 1,560,001 - 2,340,000 | 30% |
| Over 2,340,000 | 40% |

#### Monthly Income Tax Calculation

| Monthly Income (GYD) | Tax Rate |
|----------------------|----------|
| First 65,000 | 0% |
| 65,001 - 130,000 | 28% |
| 130,001 - 195,000 | 30% |
| Over 195,000 | 40% |

#### Personal Allowances and Credits

- **Old Age Allowance**: GYD 75,000 annually (for persons 65 years and over)
- **Disabled Person Allowance**: GYD 75,000 annually
- **National Insurance Scheme (NIS)**: Deductible up to maximum contribution limits
- **Pension Contributions**: Deductible (approved pension schemes)
- **Education Allowance**: For dependent children pursuing tertiary education
- **Medical Expenses**: Deductible for approved medical expenses exceeding 5% of chargeable income

#### PAYE (Pay As You Earn)

- Employers must withhold tax from employee wages monthly
- Remittance deadline: 15th day of the following month
- Annual reconciliation: Submit P60 forms by March 31st

### 1.2 Corporate Tax

#### Standard Corporate Tax Rates

| Business Type | Tax Rate |
|--------------|----------|
| Commercial Companies | 25% |
| Commercial Companies (Publicly Listed) | 27.5% |
| Telephone Companies | 45% |
| Commercial Banks | 30% |
| Non-Commercial Companies | 25% |
| Insurance Companies | 30% |
| Mining Companies (Non-Gold) | 30% |
| Gold Mining Companies | 40% |

- Micro enterprises may qualify for simplified tax regimes

#### Minimum Corporate Tax

- All companies must pay minimum tax of **2% of gross receipts** if calculated tax is less than this amount
- Applies even in loss-making years

### 1.3 Self-Employment Tax

- **Tax Rate**: Same progressive rates as personal income tax
- **Advance Tax Payments**: Required quarterly
- **NIS Contributions**: Self-employed persons must contribute both employee and employer portions

#### Quarterly Advance Payment Schedule

- **Q1**: By March 31st (for January-March)
- **Q2**: By June 30th (for April-June)
- **Q3**: By September 30th (for July-September)
- **Q4**: By December 31st (for October-December)

## 2. Value Added Tax (VAT)

### 2.1 Standard Rate

- **Current VAT Rate**: 14%
- Applied to most goods and services supplied in Guyana

### 2.2 VAT Registration Thresholds

| Business Type | Annual Turnover Threshold (GYD) |
|--------------|--------------------------------|
| Goods | 10,000,000 |
| Services | 6,000,000 |
| Mixed (Goods & Services) | 10,000,000 |

- Voluntary registration permitted below thresholds
- Registration required within 21 days of exceeding threshold

### 2.3 Zero-Rated Items

Zero-rated items carry 0% VAT but allow input tax recovery:

- **Food Items**:
  - Rice, flour, sugar, edible oils
  - Fresh fruits and vegetables
  - Fresh meat, poultry, and fish
  - Milk and dairy products (basic)
  - Bread

- **Education**:
  - Prescribed textbooks and educational materials
  - School uniforms

- **Medical**:
  - Pharmaceutical products on approved list
  - Medical equipment and supplies

- **Exports**:
  - Goods exported from Guyana
  - International transportation services

- **Agriculture**:
  - Agricultural inputs (fertilizers, seeds, pesticides)
  - Animal feed
  - Agricultural machinery

### 2.4 Exempt Items

Exempt items carry no VAT and do not allow input tax recovery:

- **Financial Services**:
  - Banking services (excluding bank charges)
  - Insurance services
  - Loan interest

- **Healthcare Services**:
  - Medical and dental services
  - Hospital services

- **Education Services**:
  - Tuition fees (primary, secondary, tertiary)
  - Vocational training

- **Residential Property**:
  - Residential rent
  - Sale of residential property

- **Public Transportation**:
  - Local passenger transport

- **Utilities** (specific exemptions):
  - Water supply
  - Certain electricity supplies

### 2.5 VAT Filing and Payment

- **Filing Period**: Monthly (for most registered businesses)
- **Filing Deadline**: Last day of the month following the tax period
- **Payment Deadline**: Same as filing deadline
- **Example**: January VAT return due February 28th/29th

#### Small Business VAT Filing

- Businesses with annual turnover < GYD 20,000,000 may apply for quarterly filing
- Quarterly deadlines: April 30th, July 31st, October 31st, January 31st

## 3. Property Tax

### 3.1 Rates and Assessment

Property tax is levied by municipal and local government authorities:

#### Georgetown Municipal Rates

- **Residential Property**: 1.5% - 2% of assessed value annually
- **Commercial Property**: 2% - 3% of assessed value annually
- **Industrial Property**: 2.5% - 3.5% of assessed value annually

#### Other Municipalities

- Rates vary by municipality (typically 1% - 3% of assessed value)
- New Amsterdam, Linden, Corriverton, Rose Hall have separate rate structures

### 3.2 Assessment Process

- Properties assessed by local Valuation Department
- Reassessment cycles: Every 3-5 years
- Appeals: Can be filed within 30 days of assessment notice

### 3.3 Payment Schedule

- **Annual Payment**: Typically due by March 31st
- **Quarterly Payments**: Some municipalities allow quarterly payments
- **Discount**: Up to 10% discount for early payment (by January 31st in some areas)

## 5. Tax Deadlines

### 5.1 Individual Tax Filing Deadlines

#### Employed Individuals (PAYE)

- **Employer Annual Return (P60)**: March 31st
- **Employee Tax Clearance**: April 30th (if required)
- **Additional Income Declaration**: April 30th

#### Self-Employed Individuals

- **Annual Return**: April 30th (for previous tax year)
- **Quarterly Advance Payments**: March 31st, June 30th, September 30th, December 31st
- **Final Payment**: April 30th (balance of tax due)

### 5.2 Corporate Tax Filing Deadlines

#### Standard Companies

- **Annual Return**: 4 months after fiscal year-end
- **Quarterly Advance Payments**: Due on 15th day of 4th, 7th, 10th, and 13th month of fiscal year
- **Minimum Tax**: Same deadline as annual return

#### Example for December 31st Year-End:
- Q1 Advance: April 15th
- Q2 Advance: July 15th
- Q3 Advance: October 15th
- Q4 Advance: January 15th (following year)
- Annual Return: April 30th (following year)

### 5.3 VAT Filing Periods

#### Monthly Filers (Standard)

- **Period**: Calendar month
- **Deadline**: Last day of following month
- **Payment**: Same as filing deadline

#### Quarterly Filers (Small Business)

- **Q1 (Jan-Mar)**: April 30th
- **Q2 (Apr-Jun)**: July 31st
- **Q3 (Jul-Sep)**: October 31st
- **Q4 (Oct-Dec)**: January 31st

### 5.4 Other Important Deadlines

| Tax Type | Filing Deadline | Payment Deadline |
|----------|----------------|------------------|
| PAYE (Monthly) | 15th of following month | 15th of following month |
| NIS Contributions | 15th of following month | 15th of following month |
| Withholding Tax | 15th of following month | 15th of following month |
| Property Tax | March 31st (varies by municipality) | March 31st (varies by municipality) |
| Capital Gains Tax | 30 days after disposal | 30 days after disposal |
| Travel Tax | At time of travel | At time of travel |

### 5.5 Penalties for Late Filing/Payment

#### Late Filing Penalties

- **Initial Penalty**: 10% of tax due or GYD 10,000 (whichever is greater)
- **Additional Penalty**: After 30 days, additional 5% monthly (up to maximum of 50%)
- **Default Assessment**: GRA may issue estimated assessment after 60 days

#### Late Payment Penalties

- **Interest on Late Payment**: 1.5% per month (18% per annum)
- **Calculated From**: Day after due date
- **Compounded**: Monthly on outstanding balance

#### Non-Compliance Penalties

- **Failure to Register**: GYD 100,000 to GYD 500,000
- **Failure to Issue Tax Invoice**: GYD 50,000 per offense
- **False Statement**: GYD 500,000 to GYD 2,000,000 or imprisonment
- **Obstruction of GRA Officer**: GYD 100,000 to GYD 500,000

## 6. Tax Credits and Deductions

### 6.1 Personal Allowances

| Allowance Type | Amount (GYD) | Eligibility |
|---------------|--------------|-------------|
| Old Age Allowance | 75,000 annually | Persons 65 years and over |
| Disabled Person Allowance | 75,000 annually | Certified disabled persons |
| Blind Person Allowance | 75,000 annually | Certified blind persons |

### 6.2 Personal Deductions

#### Employment-Related Deductions

- **NIS Contributions**: Full deduction of employee contributions
  - Employee rate: 5.6% of insurable earnings
  - Maximum monthly insurable earnings: GYD 280,000
  - Maximum monthly contribution: GYD 15,680

- **Approved Pension Contributions**: Deductible
  - Private pension plans (approved by Commissioner)
  - Employer-sponsored retirement plans

- **Union Dues**: Deductible for trade union members

#### Medical and Education Deductions

- **Medical Expenses**: Deductible if exceeding 5% of chargeable income
  - Must be for approved medical services
  - Prescription drugs on approved list
  - Medical insurance premiums

- **Education Expenses**: For dependent children
  - Tertiary education tuition fees
  - Books and required materials
  - Maximum limits apply

#### Other Personal Deductions

- **Life Insurance Premiums**: Up to GYD 30,000 annually
- **Mortgage Interest**: For primary residence (subject to limits)
- **Charitable Donations**: To approved charities (up to 10% of chargeable income)

### 6.3 Business Deductions

#### Allowable Business Expenses

- **Salaries and Wages**: Paid to employees
- **Rent**: Business premises rental
- **Utilities**: Electricity, water, telephone for business use
- **Professional Fees**: Legal, accounting, consulting services
- **Insurance**: Business insurance premiums
- **Repairs and Maintenance**: Current repairs (not capital improvements)
- **Bad Debts**: Proven uncollectible debts
- **Interest Expense**: On business loans
- **Advertising and Marketing**: Reasonable and necessary
- **Travel and Entertainment**: Business-related (subject to limits)
- **Vehicle Expenses**: Business use portion
- **Office Supplies**: Consumable items

#### Capital Allowances

| Asset Type | Annual Allowance Rate |
|-----------|----------------------|
| **Buildings** | |
| - Industrial Buildings | 10% |
| - Commercial Buildings | 5% |
| - Agricultural Buildings | 20% |
| **Machinery and Equipment** | |
| - General Plant & Machinery | 20% |
| - Computers and IT Equipment | 40% |
| - Motor Vehicles | 20% |
| - Heavy Equipment (Construction) | 25% |
| **Intangible Assets** | |
| - Patents, Trademarks | 20% |
| - Software | 40% |

- **Initial Allowance**: Available in year of acquisition (typically 20%)
- **Annual Allowance**: Reducing balance method
- **Accelerated Allowance**: May apply for certain priority sectors

#### Special Business Deductions

- **Research & Development**: 150% deduction for approved R&D
- **Training Expenses**: 150% deduction for approved employee training
- **Export Promotion**: Enhanced deductions for export-related expenses

### 6.4 Investment Incentives

#### Tax Holidays and Exemptions

- **New Manufacturing**: Up to 10 years tax holiday
- **Tourism Projects**: Up to 10 years tax holiday
- **Agriculture (Large Scale)**: Up to 7 years tax holiday
- **Information Technology**: Up to 5 years tax holiday

#### Criteria for Tax Holidays:
- Minimum investment thresholds
- Job creation requirements
- Technology transfer
- Export orientation
- Application and approval by Go-Invest (Guyana Office for Investment)

#### Import Duty Exemptions

- **Capital Equipment**: For approved projects
- **Raw Materials**: For manufacturing (subject to conditions)
- **Agricultural Inputs**: Seeds, fertilizers, equipment
- **Renewable Energy Equipment**: Solar panels, wind turbines

#### Special Economic Zones

- **Linden Enterprise Zone**: Special tax rates and incentives
- **Other Designated Zones**: Sector-specific incentives

### 6.5 Credits vs. Deductions

**Tax Credits** (reduce tax liability directly):
- Foreign tax credits (taxes paid abroad)
- Renewable energy credits
- Employment credits (hiring disadvantaged workers)

**Tax Deductions** (reduce taxable income):
- All allowable business expenses
- Personal deductions listed above
- Depreciation/capital allowances

## 7. GRA (Guyana Revenue Authority) Procedures

### 7.1 TIN (Taxpayer Identification Number) Registration

#### Individual TIN Registration

**Required For:**
- All employed persons
- Self-employed individuals
- Property owners
- Anyone conducting business

**Documents Required:**
- Valid national ID or passport
- Birth certificate
- Proof of address (utility bill, bank statement)
- Completed TIN application form (GRA-1)

**Registration Process:**
1. Complete TIN application form
2. Submit documents to nearest GRA office
3. Processing time: 3-5 business days
4. TIN card issued

**Online Registration:**
- Available through GRA e-Services portal
- Upload scanned documents
- Virtual appointment for verification

#### Business TIN Registration

**Required For:**
- All companies and partnerships
- Sole proprietorships
- Non-profit organizations
- Trusts and estates

**Documents Required:**
- Certificate of Incorporation (companies)
- Partnership agreement (partnerships)
- Business name registration
- Proof of business address
- Directors'/Partners' TINs
- Completed business registration form (GRA-2)

**Registration Process:**
1. Register business entity with Registrar of Companies
2. Apply for TIN within 21 days of registration
3. Submit required documents
4. Processing time: 5-7 business days

### 7.2 VAT Registration

**Registration Threshold:**
- Mandatory if turnover exceeds thresholds (see Section 2.2)
- Voluntary registration available below thresholds

**Documents Required:**
- Valid TIN
- Business registration documents
- Proof of business premises
- Business bank account details
- Projected turnover statement
- List of business activities

**Registration Process:**
1. Complete VAT registration form (VAT-1)
2. Submit supporting documents
3. GRA inspection of business premises (may be required)
4. VAT certificate issued (7-14 days)
5. VAT number assigned

**Obligations Upon Registration:**
- Issue tax invoices for all supplies
- Maintain proper VAT records
- File monthly/quarterly returns
- Charge and collect VAT

### 7.3 Filing Requirements

#### Electronic Filing (Mandatory for most taxpayers)

**GRA e-Services Portal:**
- Website: [https://gra.gov.gy/eservices](https://gra.gov.gy/eservices)
- Register for e-services account
- Link TIN to account
- File returns electronically

**Required Electronic Filings:**
- All companies (income tax, VAT)
- PAYE employers
- VAT-registered businesses
- Self-employed with income > GYD 5,000,000

#### Manual Filing (Limited categories)

- Small businesses below electronic threshold
- Pensioners and elderly taxpayers (discretionary)
- Filed at GRA offices or authorized agents

#### Record Keeping Requirements

**Minimum Retention Period: 6 years**

**Required Records:**
- Sales and purchase invoices
- Bank statements and deposit records
- Cash receipts and disbursements
- Asset registers
- Inventory records
- Payroll records
- Tax returns and assessments
- Correspondence with GRA

**Format:**
- Electronic records acceptable
- Must be readily accessible
- Backup required for electronic records

### 7.4 Payment Methods

#### Electronic Payment (Preferred)

**Online Banking:**
- Direct payment through GRA e-Services
- Integration with local banks:
  - Republic Bank
  - Demerara Bank
  - Citizens Bank
  - Scotiabank
  - GBTI

**Wire Transfer:**
- Bank details provided on assessment/return
- Include TIN and payment reference

#### In-Person Payment

**GRA Office Payment:**
- Cash (up to GYD 500,000)
- Manager's cheque
- Bank draft

**Authorized Bank Branches:**
- Payment at designated bank branches
- Obtain GRA receipt

#### Payment Plans

- Available for taxpayers facing financial hardship
- Application required with supporting documents
- Interest still accrues on outstanding balance
- Default on plan results in immediate collection action

### 7.5 Compliance and Enforcement

#### GRA Audits

**Types of Audits:**
- Desk audit: Review of filed returns
- Field audit: On-site business inspection
- Special audit: Fraud investigation

**Selection Criteria:**
- Random selection
- Industry benchmarking
- Red flags (unusual deductions, losses)
- Third-party information
- Prior non-compliance

**Taxpayer Rights During Audit:**
- Right to representation
- Right to know audit scope
- Right to appeal findings
- Confidentiality of information

#### Appeals Process

**Objection to Assessment:**
- File objection within 30 days of assessment
- State grounds for objection
- Provide supporting evidence

**Appeal Stages:**
1. **Internal Review**: GRA Commissioner review
2. **Tax Appeal Tribunal**: Independent tribunal hearing
3. **High Court**: Judicial review
4. **Court of Appeal**: Final appeal

**Time Limits:**
- Objection to GRA: 30 days from assessment
- Appeal to Tribunal: 30 days from GRA decision
- Appeal to Court: 30 days from Tribunal decision

#### Collection and Enforcement

**Collection Methods:**
- Demand notices
- Wage garnishment
- Bank account levy
- Asset seizure
- Business closure (extreme cases)
- Director liability (for company debts)

**Criminal Prosecution:**
- Tax evasion (willful non-compliance)
- Fraud and false statements
- Obstruction of GRA officers
- Penalties: Fines and/or imprisonment
